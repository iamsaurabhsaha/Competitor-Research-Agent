Here's a summary of the article:

- **Nutrition basics**: Good nutrition involves eating a balanced diet that supplies macronutrients (carbohydrates, protein, fat) and micronutrients (vitamins and minerals) to fuel the body and protect against diseases like heart disease, cancer, and diabetes.

- **Healthy foods**: Science supports plant-based diets like the Mediterranean-DASH diet, with "superfoods" such as berries, fatty fish, leafy greens, nuts, whole grains, and legumes offering the greatest health benefits.

- **Vitamins and supplements**: Most healthy adults can get essential nutrients from a balanced diet alone; supplements are mainly recommended for those with diagnosed deficiencies or conditions that prevent proper nutrient absorption.

- **Nutrition for older adults**: After age 50, proper nutrition becomes increasingly important, with quality of food mattering more than specific macronutrient ratios, and emphasis placed on eating plenty of plants, adequate protein, and minimally processed foods.

- **Key dietary principles**: All healthy eating plans share common guidelines: prioritizing plant foods, consuming adequate protein, avoiding ultra-processed foods, and limiting saturated fats, added sugars, and sodium.
