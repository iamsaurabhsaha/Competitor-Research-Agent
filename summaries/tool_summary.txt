The article, reviewed by Dr. Mallika Marshall, provides a comprehensive overview of nutrition and its importance for overall health. It explains that good nutrition — centered on a balanced, plant-based diet — fuels the body and protects against serious illnesses such as heart disease, cancer, and diabetes by supplying essential macronutrients (carbohydrates, protein, and fat) and micronutrients (vitamins and minerals). The article highlights "superfoods" like berries, fatty fish, leafy greens, nuts, olive oil, whole grains, yogurt, cruciferous vegetables, and legumes as particularly beneficial choices, and recommends using the Harvard Healthy Eating Plate as a visual guide for balanced meals. On the topic of dietary supplements, it notes that while they can be helpful for people with specific deficiencies or conditions, a well-rounded diet typically provides all the nutrients most healthy adults need. Finally, it emphasizes that proper nutrition is especially critical for adults over 50, and that all healthy eating plans share four core principles: plenty of plant foods, adequate protein, minimally processed foods, and limited saturated fats, added sugars, and sodium.
