• Over 1 billion people globally live with a mental health condition, yet mental health systems remain severely under-resourced, with wide treatment gaps worldwide.

• Mental health is shaped by a complex mix of individual, social, and environmental factors — including poverty, violence, genetics, and early childhood experiences — that can either increase risk or build resilience.

• Effective promotion and prevention strategies exist across multiple sectors (education, housing, labor, etc.), with priorities including suicide prevention, child and adolescent mental health, and workplace mental health.

• Community-based care is the preferred approach to treatment, integrating mental health into general healthcare, peer support, and non-health settings, while innovative tools like digital self-help can help bridge care gaps.

• The WHO's Comprehensive Mental Health Action Plan 2013–2030 outlines global commitments, urging countries to increase investment, reshape environments, and strengthen accessible, rights-based mental health services — though progress currently remains insufficient.
