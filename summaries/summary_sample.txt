Here's a summary of the article on nutrition:

- **Nutrition basics**: Good nutrition involves eating a balanced diet of macronutrients (carbohydrates, protein, fat) and micronutrients (vitamins and minerals) to fuel the body, maintain organ systems, and protect against diseases like heart disease, diabetes, and cancer.

- **Healthy foods**: Science supports plant-based diets, such as the Mediterranean-DASH diet, which emphasizes "superfoods" like berries, leafy greens, fatty fish, nuts, whole grains, and legumes for maximum nutritional benefits.

- **Vitamins and supplements**: For most healthy adults, a balanced diet provides sufficient nutrients; however, supplements may be necessary for those with diagnosed deficiencies, digestive disorders, or dietary restrictions like veganism.

- **Nutrition for older adults**: After age 50, nutrition becomes increasingly important; healthy eating plans should emphasize plenty of plants, adequate protein, minimally processed foods, and limited saturated fats, added sugars, and sodium.

- **Meal guidance**: A helpful visual tool, the Harvard Healthy Eating Plate, recommends filling half your plate with fruits and vegetables, a quarter with whole grains, and a quarter with lean protein at most meals.
