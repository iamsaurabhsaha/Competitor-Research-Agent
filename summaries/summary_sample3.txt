# Summary: 16 Biggest Environmental Problems of 2026

- **Climate change remains the dominant crisis**, with 2024 being the warmest year on record, driven by record-high greenhouse gas emissions that are intensifying extreme weather events, melting ice caps, raising sea levels, and accelerating ocean acidification.

- **Political and corporate backsliding is hampering climate action**, particularly in the US under Trump, where environmental regulations have been rolled back, while global climate conferences continue to fail at producing meaningful agreements on fossil fuel phase-outs.

- **Biodiversity loss, deforestation, and overfishing are devastating ecosystems**, with wildlife populations declining 68% since 1970, forests disappearing at alarming rates, and 30% of commercial fisheries classified as overfished — all driven primarily by agriculture, land-use change, and overconsumption.

- **Pollution crises — plastic, air, and textile waste — are worsening**, with 14 million tons of plastic entering oceans annually, millions dying from air pollution each year, and the fast fashion industry contributing 10% of global carbon emissions, yet international treaty negotiations continue to stall.

- **Emerging threats like AI and cobalt mining add new environmental pressures**, as AI's massive energy and water consumption grows largely unregulated, while mining for EV battery materials causes serious environmental damage and human exploitation in developing nations.
